History of Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, an enduring symbol of love and one of the world's most admired architectural masterpieces, stands gracefully and majestically on the banks of the Yamuna river in Agra,India. Built during the height of Mughal Empire.Its history interwines romance,artistry,politics and devotion making it much more than just a monument. It is a testament to human creativity and emotions.



The story of the Taj Mahal begins with the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, who reigned from 1628 to 1658. Shah Jahan was deeply in love with his wife Mumtaz Mahal, whosse name translates to "The chosen one of the palace". Mumtaz Mahal was not only Shah Jahan's beloved consort but also his trusted advisor and companion, accompanying him on even military campaigns. In 1631, while giving birth to their forteenth child, Mumtaz Mahal tragically passed away. Her death left Shah Jahan heart broken, and in his grief he vowed to create a monument so beautiful that would immortalize their love for eterninty.



Construction of Taj Mahal began in 1632 and took approximately 22 years to complete,finishing around 1653. It is estimated that over 20,000 artisans,craftsmen and labourersfrom across the Mughal Empire and beyond participated in its construction. The designs of Taj Mahal include Persian,Islamic and Indian architecture, creating a hamanous belnd of styles. The marble itself was sourced from Makrana in Rajasthan, while precious stones like jade,crystal,lapis lazuli and turquoise were laid using the pietra dura technique



In 1983,Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site and remains a global icon of love and Mughal artistry.,attracting millions of visitors every year.

The Black Taj Mahal

 source="wikipedia.org"

The term "Black Taj Mahl" refers to a mythical black marble mausoleum that was allegedly planned by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan as a twin structure to the iconic white Taj Mahal in Agra, India. Howerver, there is no concrete evidence of its existence, and modern archaelogists consider the story a myth, possibly inspired by 'Mehtab Bagh', a garden located across the Yamuna River from the Taj Mahal, which offers a symmentrical views. The term black Taj Mahal is often used locally in Burhanpur, Madhya Pradesh, for the tomb of Shahnawaz Khan, which is built using black stone and is an architectural wonder and tourist site.

Image

We do not know if the legend is true or false, but it is said that Shah Jahan intended to build a black marble mausoleum on the opposite bank of the Yamuna River, directly across from the white Taj Mahal for himself but could not because his son, Aurangzeb, overthrew him and imprisoned him in the Agra Fort. Shah Jahan spent the last years of his life in captivity, gazing at the Taj Mahal from a distance until his death in 1666.
